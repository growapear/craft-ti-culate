Ball Kneading
Sarah Chen
Taiwan

My interpretation of ball kneading using local clay from Yingge. The clay here has a unique texture that requires a gentler touch. I learned this technique from my grandmother who has been making pottery in this region for over 50 years.

Centering
Maria Rodriguez
Mexico

In our studio, centering is like a meditation. We focus on finding the clay's center while staying centered ourselves. This connection between potter and material is something I've learned through years of practice.

Pulling
Aisha Patel
India

The art of pulling walls is about feeling the clay's response to your touch. In my studio in Jaipur, we combine traditional techniques with contemporary forms, always respecting the clay's natural movement.

Pinching
James Kim
South Korea

Making ceramic beads requires a delicate balance of pressure and precision. Each bead is a tiny vessel of tradition. In our studio, we create contemporary interpretations of traditional Korean prayer beads, rolling each one by hand to maintain the connection to ancient practices.
